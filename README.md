# FIFA-Player-Performance-Data-Analysis-

## Project Overview

Modern football clubs generate and collect massive amounts of player performance data, including technical abilities, physical attributes, and player potential. Despite the availability of this data, many clubs still underutilize it when making recruitment, training, and tactical decisions.

This project focuses on analyzing FIFA player performance data to uncover actionable insights that can support data-driven football management. By transforming raw player statistics into meaningful analysis and interactive visualizations, the project demonstrates how analytics can improve player evaluation, squad development, recruitment efficiency, and long-term team strategy.

The analysis explored relationships between player attributes, overall ratings, age trends, positional performance, nationality strengths, and club-level potential growth. The findings provide practical recommendations for football recruitment departments, coaching teams, and performance analysts.

## Project Objectives

The primary objectives of this project were to:

* Analyze football player performance data to identify key factors influencing player quality and potential.
* Examine how technical, physical, and mental attributes contribute to overall player ratings.
* Identify age groups with the highest development potential.
* Evaluate positional strengths and performance trends.
* Explore club and nationality patterns associated with player growth and technical excellence.
* Provide data-driven recommendations for recruitment, scouting, training, and transfer market strategies.

## Tools Used

* Microsoft Excel — Data cleaning, preparation, and preliminary analysis.
* Microsoft Power BI — Data modeling, dashboard creation, and visualization.

# Dataset Description

The dataset contains detailed FIFA player information, including:

* Player overall ratings
* Potential ratings
* Player positions
* Age
* Nationality
* Club affiliation
* Preferred foot
* Technical attributes (passing, dribbling, finishing, etc.)
* Mental attributes (reactions, composure, vision, etc.)
* Physical attributes

## Data Cleaning and Preparation

Several preprocessing steps were carried out to improve the quality and usability of the dataset:

* Removed duplicate records and unnecessary columns.
* Handled missing and inconsistent values.
* Standardized player positions and categorical fields.
* Converted numerical fields into appropriate data types.
* Created calculated measures and averages for analysis in Power BI.
* Structured the dataset for efficient dashboard reporting and visualization.

## Dashboard Development

An interactive dashboard was developed in Power BI to visualize player performance trends and support decision-making.

The dashboard includes:

* Overall player rating analysis
* Potential growth analysis
* Position-based performance comparison
* Age and development trends
* Nationality analysis
* Club performance insights
* Attribute correlation analysis

The visualizations were designed to make complex football data easier to interpret for recruitment teams, analysts, and coaches.

# Key Findings and Observations

* Left Forwards (LF) recorded the highest average overall rating at 73.7.
* Central Midfielders (CM) recorded the lowest average overall rating at 61.63.
* The LF position performed approximately 19.85% higher than CM in average overall rating.

This suggests that attacking wide players in the dataset tend to possess stronger overall player profiles compared to central midfielders.

## Age and Potential Growth

* Players between the ages of 16 and 26 demonstrated the highest average potential growth.
* Player potential peaks around age 17, with an average potential rating of 74.3.
* Potential consistently declines after the age of 25.

This indicates that younger players offer the highest long-term development value and investment opportunities.

## Club-Level Potential Analysis

The clubs with the highest average potential growth were:

1. Juventus — 85.55
2. Napoli — 83.44
3. Inter Milan — 81.75

These clubs consistently develop or recruit players with high growth potential.

Nationality-Based Potential Analysis

## Countries with the highest average potential growth include:

* Dominican Republic — 80.50
* Chad — 78.00
* United Arab Emirates — 78.00

These nations displayed unexpectedly high potential averages relative to player representation.

## Correlation Analysis

The project identified strong relationships between certain attributes and overall player ratings.

Strongest Correlated Attributes

* Reactions → r = 0.85
* Composure → r = 0.73

These mental attributes showed stronger relationships with overall ratings than many technical or physical attributes.

## Trainable High-Impact Attributes

* Short Passing → r = 0.50
* Vision → r = 0.50

These attributes demonstrated strong influence on player performance while remaining highly trainable.

# Recommendations

## Recruitment Strategy

Football clubs should prioritize mental attributes such as Reactions and Composure during player recruitment. Since these attributes strongly correlate with overall player quality, they can help identify undervalued talents even at lower transfer costs.

Recruitment models should place greater emphasis on mental intelligence and decision-making rather than relying solely on physical or technical metrics.

## Training and Player Development

Clubs should invest in development programs focused on:

* Short Passing
* Vision
* Tactical awareness

Improving these trainable attributes can systematically raise overall squad performance more efficiently than focusing exclusively on finishing or attacking output.

## Youth Scouting Strategy

The analysis shows that players aged 16–20 provide the highest growth potential and long-term investment value.

Examples include:

* Vinícius Júnior — Age 17, Potential 92
* Jadon Sancho — Age 18, Potential 89

Clubs should aggressively scout and secure young high-potential talents before market values significantly increase.

## Nationality Recruitment Focus

The analysis revealed strong technical profiles among players from:

* Brazil
* Spain
* Argentina

Brazil and Spain lead in:

* Short Passing
* Dribbling

Argentina leads in:

* Finishing

These countries should serve as priority recruitment pipelines for technically dominant attacking and midfield players.

## Transfer Market Strategy

Juventus and Napoli maintain elite-quality squads while operating in relatively negotiable transfer markets compared to the English Premier League.

Italian and German leagues may therefore provide better value opportunities for clubs seeking competitive squad improvement without extreme transfer inflation.

## Left-Footed Player Advantage

Left-footed wide players create tactical width advantages and remain comparatively scarce in the market.

Clubs should be willing to pay moderate premiums for elite left-footed players because long-term supply limitations may continue to increase their strategic value.

# Conclusion

This project demonstrates how football analytics can transform raw player data into actionable intelligence for modern football management. By combining data cleaning, visualization, and statistical analysis, the project uncovered patterns in player development, recruitment value, positional performance, and attribute importance.

The findings emphasize the growing importance of data-driven decision-making in football, particularly in scouting, player development, and transfer market operations. Through Power BI dashboards and analytical insights, the project provides a practical framework for improving recruitment efficiency, maximizing player potential, and strengthening long-term team strategy.
