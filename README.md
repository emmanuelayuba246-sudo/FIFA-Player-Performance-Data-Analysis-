# FIFA-Player-Performance-Data-Analysis-
This project analyzed extensive football player performance data, including skills, potential, and physical attributes, to uncover insights that can support data-driven decisions in recruitment, training, and team strategy. By transforming underutilized player data into meaningful analysis and visualizations, the project demonstrates how analytics can improve player evaluation and overall team management.

## Tool used 
- Power BI
- Microsoft Excel

## Observations 

 - LF had the highest Average of Overall at 73.7, was 19.85% higher than CM, which had the lowest Average of Overall at 61.63.
 - Players between the ages of 16 and 26 have the highest average potential.
 - Juventus, Napoli, and Inter have the highest average potential growth of 85.55, 83.44, and 81.75 respectively.
 - Dominican Republic, Chad, and United Arab Emirates have the highest average potential growth of 80.50, 78.00, and 78.00 respectively.

# Recommendations 

## Recruitment 
Reactions (r=0.85) and Composure (r=0.73) have by far the strongest correlation with overall rating stronger than any physical or technical attribute. Clubs should screen recruits for these mental attributes first, even at lower price points, prioritize reactions and composure above all. 

## Training focus 
Short passing (r=0.50) and Vision (r=0.50) are the most trainable high-impact attributes. A coordinated passing-and-vision development program can systematically lift squad-wide ratings more efficiently than focusing on goal-scoring skills alone. 

## Scouting window 
Potential peaks at age 17 (avg 74.3) and declines consistently after 25. The 16–20 age bracket holds the deepest pool of high-potential, low-cost players. Invest early Vinícius Júnior (17, POT 92) and Sancho (18, POT 89) are textbook buys before value inflates; 16–20-year-olds offer maximum growth headroom. 

## Nationality strategy 
Brazil and Spain lead all top nations in both short passing (≈63.5) and dribbling (≈60.9, 58.1). Argentina leads in finishing. For building a technically cohesive squad, these three nations should anchor attacking and midfield recruitment pipelines. 

## Transfer market 
Juventus (avg 82.3) and Napoli (avg 80.0) hold elite squads but operate in markets with more price transparency than the Premier League. Italian and German leagues offer the best combination of squad depth and transfer negotiability for clubs looking to close the gap on elite competition. 

Left-footed wide players create natural width imbalances. Clubs should pay a modest premium for elite left-footed recruits as the supply demand gap makes them structurally undervalued over time.
